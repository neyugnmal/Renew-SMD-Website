DIGITAL COMMUNICATION & MEDIA

Students will develop cultural awareness and critical thinking skills required to critically analyze and produce a broad range of discourses across diverse professional and creative contexts, enabling them to continuously generate meaningful impact.

Overview

Digital communication, as the term implies, refers to communication mediated through digital technologies. Multimedia developers and managers utilize digital communication approaches to design and produce diverse forms of entertainment, graphic design, and artistic outputs.

Learning Outcomes

By choosing this major, students will acquire foundational knowledge of digital communication, including computer programming, remote data processing, graphic design, photography, and audio video production. Students will learn how to develop websites and create 3D animations, as well as explore CD-ROM production and computer-generated imagery. Collectively, these digital communication methods constitute the “multi” nature of multimedia. In addition, students will examine existing laws and regulations governing digital communication, as well as our roles and responsibilities in the development and application of emerging technologies.
